About Me

Having chosen the PCMB stream in class 12th, I’ve always been equally captivated by the biological intricacies of life and the logical, problem-solving beauty of mathematics. These dual fascinations have shaped my academic journey and drawn me toward a rare and powerful intersection: where life sciences meet technology.

Even as a non-CS high school graduate with limited technical training, I’ve never let that hold me back. I took the initiative to explore this interdisciplinary space hands-on—most notably by independently designing and building a working model of gel electrophoresis, a fundamental process used in DNA fingerprinting. This wasn’t just a project; it was a transformative experience. It deepened my understanding of molecular biology and awakened me to the immense power of technology in shaping scientific progress.

In the middle of my biology-driven world, I discovered something unexpected — a love for tech. That moment of discovery became a turning point. It gave me clarity about my ability to self-learn, to experiment, and to bring scientific ideas to life — all of which, I believe, deeply reflect the spirit of curiosity, initiative, and innovation that SST stands for. What drives me to create isn’t just the excitement of building new tech — it’s the empathy I’ve gained from personal experiences — especially for the healthcare workers who, despite burnout and systemic challenges, devote themselves wholeheartedly to saving lives. I feel like that is the element that fuels my desire to build solutions that matter.
